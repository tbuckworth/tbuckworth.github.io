About

I'm an AI safety researcher focused on predicting and preventing misgeneralisation of AI Agents. My work aims to develop techniques that help us detect when AI systems might pursue unintended goals, and how to prevent such failures before deployment.

Currently, I'm a MATS Fellow working on AI R&D Control Evals under Francis Rhys Ward. This research investigates how to maintain meaningful human oversight of AI systems engaged in realistic AI research and development tasks.

Before transitioning to AI safety, I spent six years working at a hedge fund in London. During that time, I learned about recursively self-improving AGI and came to believe this would be the most important development in human history. I left finance to pursue a Masters and now PhD focused on making advanced AI systems safe.

Research Interests

Goal Misgeneralisation

Developing methods to predict when RL agents will pursue proxy goals that diverge from intended objectives in novel environments.

AI Control & Oversight

Building evaluation frameworks for assessing human oversight capabilities over AI systems performing complex R&D tasks.

Neuro-Symbolic AI

Using symbolic regression to distill trained neural RL agents into interpretable policies, improving out-of-distribution generalisation.
